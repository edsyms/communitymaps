Energy in Remote Communities within British Columbia

Getting energy to the remote locations in British Columbia can be hard at times because they are so remote. "The objective of the RCE Network is to assist B.C. remote communities in implementing community energy solutions by coordinating access to network members programs. RCE Network members individual programs include support for energy education and training, community energy planning, utility service provision, clean energy development, energy efficiency and energy monitoring." (Ministry if Energy and Mines website )

In British Columbia there is close to 70 remote communities that are not connected what so ever to any of the major natural gas or electricity grids in British Columbia. Between the Province of British Columbia, the Indian and Northern Affairs Canada, BC Hydro, and First Nations Technology Council they are all working together through the Remote Community Energy Network. The main goal of this Remote Community Energy Network is to aid these remote communities in B.C. with "implementing community energy solutions by coordination access to network members' program. The Remote Community Energy Network members' individual programs include support for energy education and training, community energy planning, utility service provision, clean energy development, energy efficiency and also energy monitoring."
(Ministry if Energy and Mines website )

Renewable Energy

In one day alone there is enough sun light to power the United States of America for entire year. The climates with the most sunshine are Idaho, Montana, eastern Oregon, and also eastern Washington. Those states are the ideal locations to produce the amount of solar electricity that is needed to power entire state or even the entire country, as stated before. "The efficiencies of photovoltaic cells and solar thermal systems continue to increase slowly, with rapid cost decreases. Photovoltaic cells can be integrated with roof tiles or advanced window glazings, allowing buildings to produce more energy than they consume. Currently photovoltaic cells rely on toxic and scarce materials, are difficult to recycle, and take large amounts of energy to produce. These defects are being addressed with the current generation of cells." Renewable Energy

"Any naturally occurring, theoretically inexhaustible source of energy, as biomass, solar, wind, tidal, wave, and hydroelectric power, that is not derived from fossil or nuclear fuel." Link for Quote

"There are many sources of energy that are renewable and considered to be environmentally friendly and harness natural processes. These sources of energy provide an alternate 'cleaner' source of energy, helping to negate the effects of certain forms of pollution. All of these power generation techniques can be described as renewable since they are not depleting any resource to create the energy. While there are many large-scale renewable energy projects and production, renewable technologies are also suited to small off-grid applications, sometimes in rural and remote areas, where energy is often crucial in human development."
