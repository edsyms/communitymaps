Innovative Housing Techniques

A Short Video on Earthships

  • Grow Homes

    Grow Houses are a concept created by Avi Friedman and Witold Rybczynski from McGill University. Houses are 14' wide and can be grown to suit your needs, for example if you start a family you can easily add rooms by putting up partitions. These houses are mostly in Townhouse format and can be found in Eastern Europe as well as Canada.

    Go to Grow Home Location

  • Earthship

    Sustainable houses called Earthships are a concept created by Mike Reynolds. To make one you would start your foundation by using old tires and packing open spaces with dirt then stacking the tires to create the walls of the house. These houses are completely sustainable, having composting toilets, water filtration systems and solar panels. One could live comfortably "off the grid".

    Go to Earthship Location

  • Yurt Home

    A Yurt home can be found in the very small community of Oakhill, Nova Scotia, Canada. Yurt homes are fairly inexpensive and quick to put up and take down, you could buy some land and build your yurt. There is a community in Eastern Europe that is made up entirely of Yurt homes, these are fairly rustic living quarters in this community so it may not be for everyone!

    Go to Yurt Location

  • Narrow Home Built in Germany: Build where you can!

    The planning regulations left only 3.5 meters of building width between houses in Hohenecken, Germany...so they built a house that was 3 metres wide and put it up on stilts. This is a great example of making use of what space you have.

    Go to Narrow House Location

  • Northern Living...REALLY North

    Homes designed specifically for northern living are not something most of us would think of. In Dawson, Yukon environmental elements must be considered: windows on the south facing side, two entries (summer and winter with the winter one having an airlock), solar panels on the south facing side, cool room for sewing skins (these homes were built in a Native community), etc..

    Go to Northern Home Location

  • Want to Live in a Shipping Container?

    A container home was built in the Tahoe National Forest. Three containers were used to construct this very remotely located home. These homes can have a very modern look to them: clean, classic and contemporary.

    Go to Container Home Location

  • Consider Your Residents

    One thing that is being considered when building new residences is the quality of life those living there will want. Some complexes offer amenities such as bueaty salons, gyms, and outdoor space. It's just about WHERE you live but HOW you live as well.

    Go to Location of Parkview Terrace Affordable Housing Complex

  • Straw: Not just for farm animals

    Why build with straw and not just feed it to the animals? Straw acts as a great insulator, saving you money on home heating and cooling costs. It is also a great sound-proofer, if you live near a busy street the noise from that street would be greatly reduced. Straw bale houses are another environmentally friendly form of construction, no forests need be cut down to make lumber for construction.

    Go to Strawbale House Location

  • Repurpose Old Buildings

    A lot of cities have many old buildings that are not being used. In Portland, OR, they tool an old building that was previously used as a railroad hotel and turned it into affordable housing and several new store fronts. Restaurants came to the area too, thus the city not only created affordable housing but creating a desireable place for people to spend time.

    Go to former RailRoad Hotel Location

  • Tiny House!

    This tiny house is the residence of a family of 4...in 320 sq ft! Tiny houses tend to be self-contained, and leave a tiny environmental footprint. Most tiny home builders tend to use recycled/reclaimed materials, which makes these homes another example of environmentally friendly living.

    Go to Tiny House Location
