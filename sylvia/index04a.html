Key Concepts

Complete & Safe Streets

Complete and safe streets increase active transportation and in turn increase health, resilience and sustainability throughout the community. The street design can include features such as bike lanes, pedestrian and bike barriers, traffic calming design, curb bulbouts and medians, mixed use areas, community gathering spaces, multimodal access, and shared use spaces.

Explore this concept

Green Infrastructure

Streets designed to include green infrastructure for storm water management save money and ecosystems. Storm water runoff can pick up harmful substances such as road salt, heavy metals and oils. These substances end up in streams or lakes, where they can damage water quality and aquatic habitat. Integrated storm water management systems, such as rain gardens, tree wells, porous pavement, gravel interceptors and biofiltration swales are landscaped features adapted to provide onsite treatment of storm water runoff. When rain water is detained and allowed to slowly permeate into the ground, flooding and pollution are deceased.

Explore this concept

Beautiful & Innovative Design

Beautifully designed streets encourage more walking, local spending, and community connections. Design features can include street art, plantings, unique vistas, water fountains, parks, plazas, al fresco dining spaces, sculptures, seating areas, and unique architecture. Streetscapes that are distinctive and memorable attract pedestrians and make walking more enjoyable.

Explore this concept

Street Design That Helps Foster Prosperous, Livable Communties

Street design can help foster communities that are more resilient, livable, safe, and healthy. Many aspects contribute to an improved street design. These include: active transportation routes, certification programs, new technology, design guidebooks, green infrastructure, traffic calming devices, and policies.
