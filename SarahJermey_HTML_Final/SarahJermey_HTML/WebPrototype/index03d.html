    Welcome to Gold River Reserve, in Cheaster Basin, Nova Scotia . Known for their yearly PowWow and great Health Center. Find out more about Gold River!

  • Yarmouth Reserve

    The Yarmouth Reserve is located right outside the Town of Yarmouth. It is a well populated community that has many resources for youth and adults for phyical actives and personal well being. These are localed inside and outside of the community with the ability to accsess the urban area minutes away! Find out more about Yarmouth!

  • Wildcat Community

    The Wildcat Reserve is very rural community in Queens County. Despite the location they have a great deal of opportunity and resource for promoting active living and personal wellbeing. Find out more about Wildcat Community!

    Click to see Wildcat Community on the map

  • Ponhook Reserve

    Ponhook Reserve is the most rural reserve Acadia owns. Nested on the shore of Lake Rossignal. Also a neightbouring the begining of the Nova Scotia Power Hydro-Dam. Although the land is greatly scarred from the effects of the dam, it is a nice area to gather spiritual well being. Find out more about Ponhook Reserve!

    Click here to see Ponhook Reserve on the map

  • Shelburne Sub-Office

    Although Shelburne is not reserve land, the sub-office is there to assist our off reserve members with support and information. These people most generally live in or around the Shelburne area. Find out more about the Shelburne Sub-Office!

    Click here to see Shelburne Sub-Office on the map

  • Hammonds Plains Office

    The Hammonds Plains Office is almost the same as the Shelburne location, but they have their own property even though it is not classified as reserve land. Find out more about Hammonds Plains Office!

    Click here to see Hammonds Plains Office on the map

  • Medway Reserve

    Medway Reserve is the smallest reserve land that is owned by Acadia. Although they are small, they have a lot to offer and and great resources at their community office. http://www.acadiafirstnation.ca

    Find out more about the Medway Reserve!

  • Island Trail-Wildcat

    This trail was developed in the early 2000's by the community members of Wildcat. At first, the vision was to develop a beautiful trail around the edges and through the heart of the island with many camp sites and out-houses. With not much maintenance throughout the years, the trial has grown up a fair mount and no out-houses left. http://www.acadiafirstnation.ca

    Find out more about Wildcat!

  • Gold River PowWow Grounds

    The Gold River PowWow Grounds is a sacred place where annual PowWow gatherings happen in September. It is open to the public and anyone is welcome. There is great food, wonderful songs, drumming, and dancers, and many venders selling traditional items. http://www.facebook.com

    Check out their Facebook page!

  • Tkipok Trail- Arcadia

    The summer 2016, Acadia First Nation helped and supported the restoration of the new Tkipok Trail that is around and behind the Arcadia Consolidated School. The trail has interpretation panels to teach you some history of the land and also has a beautiful board walk. http://www.thevanguard.ca

    Click here to read about Acadia's hand in the new trail!
