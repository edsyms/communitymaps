Community

A cultural map has the potential to benefit the local region in a number of ways:

  • Increase awareness of cultural sector
  • Identify key components of the cultural sector
  • Provide up-to-date information for locals, organizations and visitors
  • Locate cultural gaps, needs, networks and hubs
  • Provide a foundation for municipal cultural planning
  • Give visibility to intangible benefits of culture
  • Promote local cultural organizations, businesses and entrepreneurs
  • Provide a visual representation of networking/partnering potential
  • Contribute to pride of place/ sense of cultural community
  • Enhance the attractiveness of the region for immigrants, tourists, entrepreneurs, and small businesses

The Kings County Cultural Mapping project is supported by…..

The Community – First, and most significantly, the project emphasizes the importance of local knowledge and community input. The project could not exist without active participation from a cross-section of community members affecting both the process and the end product.

An Advisory Committee - The creation of the map was supported by a 12 person Advisory Committee, which was comprised of cultural stakeholders from across Kings County, most of whom wear many different hats in the cultural sector.

Stakeholders -The project has garnered support from local and regional organizations such as the Kings Regional Development Agency, the Alliance of Kings Artists, the Wolfville Rotary, and the Municipality of the County of Kings in terms of hosting the final product, offering resources and information, and/or contributing financially.

Mapping Technicians– The map was designed and created by Ed Symons and his 2012 Community Mapping class at the Centre Of Geographic Sciences. As well, the Municipality of the County of Kings provides ongoing maintenance of the map.
