Deicing Research Project

A key component of the Community and Environmental Planning concentration at COGS is an industry partnered project. Considering the working relationship established with the Construction Engineering section at 14 Wing, CFB Greenwood last spring, the decision was made to continue working together for this research project. One of the routine safety procedures carried out on the base relating to wing operations is aircraft deicing. Aircraft deicing is important to flight and passenger sagrty, though can be harmful to the environment. This is because chemical fluids applied to aircraft are running off into drainage systems and groundwater, contaminating these resources.
The project will involve selecting ideal areas to deice aircraft and to determine suitable areas to install automated glycol sampling sites. These decisions will be based on environmental sustainability. Overall, the purpose of the project is to look at appropriate changes to move forward with deicing in the interest of flight safety, though also in the interest of environmental sustainability.
