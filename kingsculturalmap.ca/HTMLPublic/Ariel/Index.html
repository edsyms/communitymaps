Research Project

A key component of the Community and Environmental Planning program at COGS is an industry partnered project. Considering the working relationship established with the Construction Engineering Section at 14 Wing, CFB Greenwood last spring, the decision was made to continue working together for this project. One of the routine safety procedures carried out on the base relating to wing operations is aircraft deicing. Aircraft deicing is important to flight and passenger safety, though can be harmful to the environment. This is because chemical fluids applied to aircraft are running off into drainage systems and groundwater, contaminating these resources.
Throughout my project, I intend to select ideal areas to deice aircraft and determine suitable areas to install automated chemical sampling sites. These decisions will be based on environmental sustainability. Background research and writing, GIS deliverables, client meetings and strong time management will be necessary to achieve my project goals.
