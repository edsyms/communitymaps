Research Project

The beautiful, picturesque Kings County, that many residents call home, is a gem of Nova Scotia. With Nova Scotia being mostly surrounded by water, the 6700 kilometre-long coastline is an important area that we need to ensure is protected.

According to National Geographic, the Global Mean Sea Level has risen by 4 to 8 inches over the past century. Though reflecting over the past two decades the annual rate of sea level rise has been 0.13 inches per year, about twice the average speed of the previous 80 years.

Coastal Erosion is an issue for all coastal Countries, Provinces, States and the like. By digitizing the shoreline over the past 50 or more years, using satellite imagery or aerial photography, we can assess the current erosion rate and use model to approximately predict the future rate of erosion, this will give a visual representation of the issue at hand.

With the knowledge that we can gain from this report, Kings County can protect areas along the coast that have severe erosion rates, so that no development will be permitted.
